Top sights

Temple Location:

Sri Mukhalingeswara Temple is located in Mukhalingam Village of Srikakulam District, Andhra Pradesh. which is located at a distance of about 48 km from Srikakulam and 178 km from Visakhapatnam.

Temple History:

This temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is said to have been constructed during the 8th century by Kamarnava-II when the region was under the control of the East Ganga Dynasty. It is one of the oldest temples in the region. Apart from being a site of historical significance, it is noted for its architectural beauty, which comprise intricate designs, commendable reliefs and wonderful domes.

Temple Location:

Arasavalli Sun Temple is located in Arasavalli Village, which is about 3 km from Srikakulam Town in Andhra Pradesh.

Temple History:

Dedicated to Lord Surya (the Sun God), this temple is said to have been built in the 7th century by King Devendra Varma of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. It is a rare and unique temple devoted to the Sun God, featuring ancient inscriptions and artistic sculptures. It is believed that the temple was designed to allow the first rays of the sun to fall directly on the deity.

Temple Location:

Sri Kurmanatha Swamy Temple is situated in Srikurmam Village of Srikakulam District, Andhra Pradesh. It is located approximately 13 km from Srikakulam Town and 100 km from Visakhapatnam.

Temple History:

This temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his Kurma (tortoise) incarnation. It is an ancient temple constructed during the 11th century by the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. The temple is noted for its unique depiction of Lord Vishnu in the form of a tortoise and its intricate carvings on the pillars and walls. It is also considered one of the most significant Vaishnavite shrines in South India.
